The Dual Erosion Timeline

Two interconnected but distinct pathways with different causes, timelines, and intervention windows

‚ö†Ô∏è

PATH 1: Organizational Burnout

Months ‚Üí Early Years (PRIMARY PATHWAY)

Industrial-Organizational Psychology

Definition

A work-related syndrome of emotional exhaustion, cynicism, and reduced efficacy‚Äîdriven by job design, workload, leadership, culture, and recovery deficits, not individual weakness.

Onset

Months to 1-3 years (not 10-15). Burnout emerges early when demands exceed resources.

Primary Predictors (IO Research)
  • Job Demands-Resources: Bakker & Demerouti (2007, 2014); Crawford et al. (2010)
  • Emotional Labor: H√ºlsheger & Schewe (2011); Brotheridge & Lee (2002)
  • Role Stressors: Lee & Ashforth (1996); Kahn et al. (1964)
  • Conservation of Resources: Hobfoll (1989, 2001)
  • Effort-Recovery Model: Meijman & Mulder (1998)
  • Organizational > Operational Stress: Shane (2010); Violanti & Aron (1995)
Intervention Window

Immediate (0-3 years)
Strengthen meaning, coping capacity, recovery, identity resilience, and leadership support. Prevent early trajectory decline.

‚Üí THIS IS WHERE BETTERUP HAS GREATEST IMPACT

üíî

PATH 2: Clinical Deterioration

Years 5-10+ (SECONDARY PATHWAY)

Trauma & Clinical Psychology

Definition

PTSD, depression, suicidality, sleep dysregulation, and physiological decline‚Äîdriven by cumulative trauma, chronic strain, moral injury, isolation, and stigma.

Onset

Years 5-10: Peak vulnerability
Years 10-15+: Chronic impairment, suicide risk

Primary Predictors (Clinical Research)
  • Syed et al. (2020): Global PTSD, depression, suicidality prevalence
  • BCOPS (2025): 12-year physiological deterioration study
  • Hansen et al. (2025): Exposure-response PTSD risk
  • Moral Injury: Betrayal, value conflict, operational trauma
  • Montreal Police: 22-year suicide prevention success
Intervention Window

Mid-career (Years 3-10): Prevention
Years 10+: Treatment, crisis, rehabilitation

The Largest Study of Its Kind

Under Jeff Lang's leadership, WSIB (Ontario's Workplace Safety & Insurance Board) and BetterUp Labs launched a five-year randomized controlled trial‚Äîextending support to roughly 5 million workers across Ontario, including police officers, firefighters, and healthcare workers.

The trial uses a waitlist control design: half receive coaching immediately, the other half later‚Äîenabling researchers to assess causality while ensuring everyone benefits from the intervention.
